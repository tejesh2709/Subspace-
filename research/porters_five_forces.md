# Porter's Five Forces — Subspace.money

## Framework Overview

Porter's Five Forces analyses the structural attractiveness of the market Subspace competes in and identifies where the company's moats are strongest and most vulnerable.

---

## Force 1 — Competitive Rivalry 🔴 HIGH

### Key Rivals
| Competitor | Overlap Area | Threat Level |
|---|---|---|
| CRED | Subscription tracking, fintech ecosystem | High |
| Splitwise | Group splitting, expense management | High |
| Google Pay | Recurring payment tracking, splitting | Medium |
| myPaisaa / MoneyClub | Direct subscription management | Medium |
| PhonePe | Payment rails, potential mini-app competitor | Medium |

### Why Rivalry is High
- **Low switching cost:** Users can move between apps instantly with no financial penalty
- **Multi-homing is common:** Most users already have GPay + Splitwise + a banking app. Adding Subspace doesn't replace any of them
- **No dominant category winner yet:** India's subscription management space has no clear leader, making it actively contested
- **Feature overlap is increasing:** CRED's Money product now tracks subscriptions. GPay shows recurring mandates. The feature gap is narrowing

### Subspace's Competitive Response
- Negotiate API is genuinely differentiated — no rival can surface "pay ₹149 instead of ₹649" at point of detection
- Bootstrapped profitability means Subspace doesn't need to win a VC-funded acquisition war
- India-first positioning (local languages, local brands) is a moat CRED and Splitwise haven't fully exploited

---

## Force 2 — Threat of New Entrants 🟡 MEDIUM

### Low Barriers
- Any fintech startup can build a subscription tracker in 3–6 months
- Open banking / AA framework lowers data access barriers
- App distribution on Play Store / App Store is frictionless

### High Barriers
- **Negotiate API:** Years of BD relationships with OTT and service providers to negotiate group rates. Not replicable quickly
- **Data flywheel:** Subspace's proprietary conversation and transaction intelligence compounds with scale — a new entrant starts from zero
- **Bootstrapped unit economics:** Subspace has optimised ops to profitability without external capital, giving it pricing flexibility a funded entrant doesn't have

### Most Credible New Entrant Threat
**PhonePe or Paytm** — if either decided to build subscription management natively, they'd have instant distribution (600M users) and existing payment data. This is the existential new entrant risk Subspace needs to address via a partnership strategy (see Feedback #4) before it becomes a competitive threat.

---

## Force 3 — Threat of Substitutes 🔴 HIGH

### Direct Substitutes
| Substitute | How it replaces Subspace | Switching friction |
|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp + GPay | Informal bill splitting in existing apps | Zero |
| Bank app recurring mandates view | Basic subscription visibility | Zero |
| Google Sheets / Notion | Manual subscription tracking | Low |
| Direct OTT family plans | Eliminates the sharing need entirely | Low |
| Asking family to share account | Free, informal subscription sharing | Zero |

### Why Substitute Threat is High
The core jobs Subspace does — track subscriptions, split bills, save on OTT — all have informal workarounds that cost the user nothing and require no new app. Subspace must deliver **significantly more value** than the informal alternative to justify the download and onboarding friction.

### Subspace's Defence
The Negotiate API is the only substitute-proof feature — no informal workaround delivers "verified 75% discount on Netflix with guaranteed access." Everything else (tracking, splitting) has a free substitute.

---

## Force 4 — Buyer Power 🔴 HIGH

### Why Buyers Have Power
- **Zero switching cost:** Delete the app, done. No cancellation fee, no data lock-in, no network dependency for solo users
- **Free tier:** No financial commitment means no financial reason to stay
- **Abundant alternatives:** Multiple apps cover each individual feature
- **Low engagement frequency:** Subscriptions are set-and-forget. Users may open Subspace once a month at most, reducing habit formation

### Subspace's Response
The savings dashboard (Feedback #5) and Sharing Guarantee (Feedback #1) are both designed to increase perceived switching cost — if you've saved ₹3,200 through Subspace this month, the emotional cost of leaving is higher than a blank slate.

Group features also increase switching cost: if your flatmates are settled on Subspace for rent splitting, leaving means disrupting the group.

---

## Force 5 — Supplier Power 🟡 MEDIUM

### Key Suppliers
| Supplier | Dependency | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| OTT Platforms (Netflix, Prime, Hotstar) | Core subscription sharing inventory | 🔴 HIGH |
| NPCI / UPI Rails | Payment settlement infrastructure | 🟢 LOW |
| Telecom providers (SMS data) | Auto-detection data source | 🟡 MEDIUM |
| Account Aggregator gateways | Bank data for detection | 🟡 MEDIUM |
| App stores (Google, Apple) | Distribution | 🟡 MEDIUM |

### OTT Platform Risk (Critical)
OTT platforms are simultaneously Subspace's core suppliers **and** potential competitors/blockers. Netflix, Disney+, and Amazon Prime have:
- The ability to enforce device limits and block shared logins
- The incentive to do so (they want full-price subscribers)
- Already demonstrated willingness globally (Netflix 2023–24 crackdown)

This is the highest supplier power risk in Subspace's business model and forms the basis of Feedback #1.

### Mitigation
- Migrate inventory to officially sanctioned group/family plans
- Diversify beyond OTT into software subscriptions (Adobe, Microsoft 365, Canva) where crackdown risk is lower
- Build direct relationships with local Indian subscription providers via the marketplace (lower supplier power, higher margin)

---

## Overall Assessment

| Force | Level | Key Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive Rivalry | 🔴 High | Must win on the Negotiate API moat — feature parity alone won't win |
| New Entrants | 🟡 Medium | Partnership with PhonePe before PhonePe becomes the competitor |
| Substitutes | 🔴 High | Every feature needs to be 10x better than the informal workaround |
| Buyer Power | 🔴 High | Increase switching cost via savings anchoring and group dependency |
| Supplier Power | 🟡 Medium | OTT crackdown is the single biggest unhedged structural risk |

**Bottom line:** Subspace is in a structurally challenging market but has a genuine, defensible moat in the Negotiate API. The strategic priority should be deepening that moat (via PhonePe distribution and AA integration) before rivals or OTT platforms erode the foundation.
