# Feedback #5 — UX

## 🔴 The Home Screen Tries to Do Everything — and Communicates Nothing

---

### (a) Observed

When you land on Subspace's home screen, you're immediately hit with multiple competing sections visible above the fold or within the first scroll:

- Subscription deals & discounts
- Gift card offers
- Rental listings
- Group sharing prompts
- Expense tracking widgets
- Marketplace browse sections
- Promotional banners

There is **no clear visual hierarchy** guiding the eye to a primary action. A first-time user cannot immediately answer:
> *"What is the one thing I should do right now?"*

Subspace's actual moats — the Negotiate API and auto-detection — are buried underneath promotional gift card banners that look identical to CashKaro, GrabOn, or Meesho. The app looks like a deals aggregator, not a subscription intelligence platform.

---

### (b) Problem

This is a classic **feature-dumping UX anti-pattern** — every team ships their feature onto the home screen and nobody owns the complete first-time user journey.

**Three compounding effects:**

**1. New user confusion**
A user who downloaded Subspace to "save on Netflix" lands on a screen that looks like a mashup of Meesho, CashKaro, and Splitwise. No clear starting point → many users don't start at all.

**2. Core value dilution**
Subspace's strongest hook: *"We save you money on subscriptions automatically."*
That message is invisible on the home screen. Gift cards and rental listings compete with — and visually outrank — the subscription savings feature that drives the actual value proposition.

**3. 90-second retention cliff**
Users who don't find value in the first 90 seconds of an app rarely return. Subspace is a multi-feature app competing against single-purpose apps (Splitwise only splits, Netflix only streams). It needs its home screen to work twice as hard to establish a clear identity — and right now it doesn't.

---

### (c) Ship Instead

#### Fix 1 — Intent-Based Onboarding (One Question)
Ask during signup:
> *"What brought you to Subspace?"*
> - 💰 Save on subscriptions
> - 👥 Split bills with friends
> - 📦 Rent something

Build the home screen around that answer. A user who came to save on OTT sees their detected subscriptions and savings opportunities front and center — not gift cards.

**Reference:** Duolingo, Headspace, and Zepto all personalise the home screen based on a single onboarding intent question. It's low engineering effort, high impact.

#### Fix 2 — One Dominant CTA on Home
Replace the cluttered feed with a single hero card:
> *"You're spending ₹2,400/month on subscriptions. Save up to ₹1,800 →"*

Everything else — marketplace, rentals, gift cards — moves to a clearly labelled bottom nav tab. This mirrors how Swiggy and Zepto handle multi-category: one dominant use case on home, everything else one tap away.

#### Fix 3 — Sticky Savings Dashboard as Top Card
Show a running monthly total:
> *"You've saved ₹3,200 this month with Subspace 🎉"*

This creates an **emotional anchor** that makes the home screen feel personal and valuable — not a promotions feed. It reinforces the core brand promise every single time the app is opened and gives users a reason to come back.

---

### Before / After Summary

| Element | Current State | Proposed State |
|---|---|---|
| Home screen focus | 6+ competing sections | 1 dominant CTA based on intent |
| First action clarity | None | Immediate — personalised to signup intent |
| Brand promise visibility | Buried | Hero card, always visible |
| Moat visibility | Hidden | Auto-detection results front and center |
| Emotional hook | None | Running savings total |
